# Chengdu & Leshan Food Guide

---

## PART 1: CHENGDU (成都)

Chengdu is a UNESCO City of Gastronomy and the capital of Sichuan cuisine. The food here is defined by **ma la** (麻辣) -- the numbing-spicy combination of Sichuan peppercorns and chili peppers -- plus fermented bean paste (doubanjiang, 豆瓣酱), which forms the soul of many dishes.

---

### 1. HOTPOT (火锅)

Chengdu-style hotpot uses beef tallow (niuyou, 牛油) as the base, giving it a richer, heavier flavor than Chongqing-style. You pick raw ingredients and cook them in a boiling, spicy broth at your table.

#### Top Hotpot Restaurants

|---|---|---|---|

| **Xiaolongkan** | 小龙坎火锅 | Multiple locations (Chunxi Road most popular) | Originated in Chongqing; picture menus and English support; good first hotpot experience |
| **Shu Jiu Xiang** | 蜀九香火锅 | Multiple locations | Loved by locals for authentic spicy broth; English menu available |

**Must-order hotpot items:** Duck intestine (鸭肠), beef tripe (毛肚), sliced beef (嫩牛肉), duck blood (鸭血), bamboo shoots (竹笋), tofu skin (豆皮), potato slices (土豆片).

### 2. CHUAN CHUAN XIANG -- Skewer Hotpot (串串香)

Skewers of meat, vegetables, and tofu on bamboo sticks, cooked in a communal spicy broth. ~1-3 RMB per skewer -- one of the most economical Chengdu meals.

#### Top Chuan Chuan Restaurants

---

### 3. BOBO JI -- Cold Pot Skewers (钵钵鸡)

Skewered chicken, lotus root, and vegetables soaked in a clay pot of cold spicy red chili oil. Light, refreshing, perfect for summer. **Originally from Leshan** (see Part 2), but widely available in Chengdu.

- Available at street stalls in **Kuanzhai Alley** and throughout the city
- Look for vendors with the characteristic clay pots (钵钵)

### 5. ICONIC SICHUAN DISHES & WHERE TO EAT THEM

#### Mapo Tofu (麻婆豆腐)
Silky tofu in a fiery sauce of doubanjiang (豆瓣酱), chili oil, ground beef, and Sichuan peppercorns. Invented in the Qing Dynasty by Lady Chen.

**Chen Mapo Tofu** (陈麻婆豆腐) -- THE original restaurant, est. ~1860s:
- **Headquarters:** No. 51 Donghuamen Street, Qingyang District
- **Qinghua Road branch:** No. 10 Qinghua Road, Qingyang District (near Qingyang Taoist Station, Line 5) -- Michelin Guide listed
- **Luomashi branch:** 197 Xi Yu Long Jie, Luomashi, Qingyang District
- Hours: 11:00-14:30, 17:00-21:00 -- go early, long queues

#### Dan Dan Noodles (担担面)
Thin wheat noodles with spicy sesame paste, minced pork, preserved vegetables (yacai 芽菜), scallions, and chili oil. Small portions, explosive flavor.

- **Ma Wang Zi Dan Dan Mian** (麻王子担担面) -- local favorite, balance of nutty and spicy
- **Mi Xun Teahouse** (觅寻茶室) at The Temple House hotel -- upscale version in tranquil setting

#### Kung Pao Chicken (宫保鸡丁)
Originated in Chengdu during Qing Dynasty. Diced chicken with peanuts, dried chilies, Sichuan peppercorns. The authentic version is bolder and spicier than international versions.

- **Yu's Family Kitchen** (喻家厨房) -- fine-dining creative take on Sichuan classics; reservations needed

#### Fuqi Feipian (夫妻肺片) -- "Husband and Wife Lung Slices"
Thinly sliced beef and offal in spicy chili oil sauce with Sichuan peppercorn.

- **Guo's Feipian** -- still run by descendants of the original creators

#### Twice-Cooked Pork (回锅肉)
Pork belly sliced, boiled, then stir-fried with doubanjiang and leeks/garlic sprouts.

- **Shizilou Restaurant** (狮子楼) -- known for traditional Sichuan classics
- Family-style versions at local eateries around Kuanzhai Alley

#### Sichuan Cold Noodles (凉面 / 鸡丝凉面)
Cold wheat noodles with sesame paste, chili oil, vinegar, sugar, garlic. Variations include:
- **Jisi Liangmian** (鸡丝凉面) -- with shredded chicken, light and refreshing
- **Tian Shui Mian** (甜水面) -- sweet and spicy thick noodles
- Available at most street food stalls and noodle shops city-wide

#### Longchaoshou -- Sichuan Wontons (龙抄手)
- **Long Chaoshou** (龙抄手总店) -- the original chain on Chunxi Road

### 6. RABBIT HEADS (兔头)

A Chengdu specialty. Braised or spicy rabbit heads, eaten by cracking open and nibbling the tender meat. ~8 RMB per head.

| Restaurant | Chinese | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Shuangliu Laoma Tutou** | 双流老妈兔头 | Shuangliu district (near airport) is THE rabbit head hotspot |
| **Meiji Rabbit Head** | 美记兔头 | Another Shuangliu legend |
| **Lao Ma Tutou** | 老妈兔头 | Available city-wide |

Flavors: spiced/braised (五香) or spicy (麻辣). Start with five-spice if you're new to it.

### 7. STREET FOOD STREETS & AREAS

#### Jinli Ancient Street (锦里古街)
- Adjacent to Wuhou Shrine (武侯祠)
- Most famous food street; touristy but atmospheric
- Signature snacks: **San Da Pao** (三大炮, sticky rice balls -- noisy preparation), **Tang You Guozi** (糖油果子, fried sticky rice balls with brown sugar & sesame), **Dan Hong Gao** (蛋烘糕, folded egg cake), sweet water noodles (甜水面), buckwheat noodles
- Higher prices than elsewhere; worth visiting for atmosphere

#### Kuanzhai Xiangzi -- Wide and Narrow Alleys (宽窄巷子)
- Qing Dynasty architecture mixed with modern dining
- Traditional Sichuan snacks, hotpot restaurants, teahouses
- More polished/tourist-oriented but still excellent

#### Yulin Neighborhood (玉林)
- **THE local food area** -- most frequently recommended by residents
- Grid of older residential streets packed with small restaurants, hotpot joints, BBQ spots, late-night noodle shops
- Best density of good food per block in the city
- Where locals eat after work

#### Kuixinglou Street (魁星楼街)
- Close to Kuanzhai Alleys
- Less crowded, more budget-friendly than Kuanzhai
- Traditional to modern to international cuisine

#### Jianshelu / Jianshe Middle Road (建设中路)
- Famous for chuan chuan and street food stalls

#### Sansheng Street (三圣街)
- Few minutes from Chunxi Road
- One of the few surviving old neighborhoods in city center
- Family-run eateries serving chaoshou (抄手, Sichuan wontons), boboji, qiaojiao niurou, homestyle dishes

#### Chunxi Road (春熙路)
- Major shopping/dining pedestrian street
- More commercial but has good restaurants including Chen Mapo Tofu branch

### 8. TEA HOUSES (茶馆)

Chengdu has more teahouses than any other city in China. The teahouse is Chengdu's "living room" -- locals gather to chat, play mahjong, listen to Sichuan opera, or simply relax. Tea typically costs just 10-20 RMB.

| Teahouse | Chinese | Location | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Heming Teahouse** | 鹤鸣茶社 | People's Park (人民公园), center of the park | Over 100 years old; jasmine tea and Sichuan green tea; bamboo chairs; ear-cleaning service available (~30 RMB); the quintessential Chengdu teahouse experience |
| **Pengzhen Old Tea House** | 彭镇老茶馆 | Pengzhen town (彭镇), ~30min from city center | One of the oldest surviving teahouses in China; incredibly photogenic; Mao-era posters on walls; time-capsule atmosphere |
| **Wenshu Monastery Teahouse** | 文殊院茶馆 | Inside Wenshu Monastery (文殊院) | Spiritual, serene setting; Tang Dynasty monastery; vegetarian snacks |
| **Shunxing Ancient Teahouse** | 顺兴老茶馆 | Near Jinsha Museum | Best for Sichuan Opera performances; also serves traditional Chengdu snacks |
| **Mi Xun Teahouse** | 觅寻茶室 | The Temple House hotel | Upscale; plant-forward Sichuan-inspired dishes; serene courtyard; fusion approach |

**Tip:** At Heming Teahouse and other traditional spots, look for the **caier** (采耳) ear-cleaning service -- a street vendor cleans your ears with tiny tools. Weirdly relaxing Chengdu tradition.

## PART 2: LESHAN (乐山)

Leshan (home of the Giant Buddha) is ~1.5 hours from Chengdu by high-speed rail and has its own distinct food culture. Many consider Leshan food even more flavorful than Chengdu's. Several dishes that are famous in Chengdu actually **originated in Leshan**.

---

### 1. SWEET SKIN DUCK (甜皮鸭)

Leshan's signature dish. The whole duck is deep-fried, then brushed with molten maltose (麦芽糖) and sesame, creating an amber, crackling sweet-crispy skin over juicy, savory meat. Dark brown color, fragrant and addictive.

#### Best Restaurants

| Restaurant | Chinese | Location | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Zhao's Duck (Zhao Yazi)** | 赵鸭子 | No. 193, south section of Chunhua Road (春华路南段), Shizhong District | Avg ~60 CNY/person; 8:30-21:00; sweeter skin, very tender meat; also has excellent braised foods |
| **Stalls at Zhanggongqiao** | 张公桥 | Zhanggongqiao Food Street | Ducks roasted and brushed with maltose in real-time; watch the preparation |

---

### 2. BOBO JI -- Cold Pot Skewers (钵钵鸡)

**Originally from Leshan!** "Bobo" (钵钵) refers to the clay pottery pot. Skewers of chicken and vegetables are cooked, then served cold in a large pot of spicy, numbing chili oil broth. You pull out whichever skewers you want. The chicken is often cooked fresh to order.

#### Best Restaurants

| Restaurant | Chinese | Location | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **WeiXiao Boboji** | 胃笑·开水鸡钵钵鸡店 | 28 Jiaxiang Road (嘉祥路), Shizhong District | Avg ~50 CNY/person; 9:00-21:00 |
| **Ye Bobo Ji** | 叶婆婆钵钵鸡 | Various locations in Leshan | Well-known traditional style |

---

### 3. QIAOJIAO BEEF (跷脚牛肉) -- "Crossed-Leg Beef"

A rich, delicate, milky-white beef broth slow-cooked from beef bones and ~30 herbs, served with various cuts of beef and offal. Named because customers historically waited so long they'd sit with legs crossed. A clear, fresh-flavored Sichuan dish (not spicy). Served with a spicy chili dipping sauce on the side.

Also available as a hot pot where you cook additional items in the broth.

#### Best Restaurants

| Restaurant | Chinese | Location | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Fang Fang Qiaojiao Beef** | 芳芳跷脚牛肉 | Central Leshan | Local institution |
| **Yi Laoba Qiaojiao Beef** | 易老八跷脚牛肉 | Central Leshan | Another local favorite |
| **GuShiXiang Qiaojiao Beef** | 古市香跷脚牛肉 | No. 85, Guihua Road (桂花路), Shizhong District | Highly recommended |

---

### 4. TOFU JELLY / TOFU BRAIN (豆腐脑)

A popular Leshan breakfast snack. Soft, silky tofu flower in a sticky, starchy sauce, topped with chili oil, soy sauce, sesame oil, peanuts, crispy fried noodles, beef bits, garlic, and celery leaves. Rich and savory -- completely different from the sweet versions found elsewhere in China.

Varieties include:
- With chicken shreds (鸡丝豆腐脑)
- With steamed beef and ground rice (牛肉豆腐脑)
- With crispy fried pork

Available at breakfast stalls throughout the city.

### 6. LESHAN FOOD STREETS

#### Zhanggongqiao Food Street (张公桥美食街)
- **THE #1 food street in Leshan** -- both locals and visitors
- Ming/Qing dynasty-inspired architecture
- Specialties: bobo chicken, tofu jelly, sweet skin duck, qiaojiao beef
- Best experienced in the evening

#### Shangzhongshun Street (上中顺街)
- Another popular food street

#### Jiaxing Road (嘉兴路)
- Good for local snacks and restaurants

---

### 7. PRACTICAL TIPS FOR LESHAN

- **Getting there:** High-speed rail from Chengdu South Station to Leshan ~1-1.5 hours
- **Day trip strategy:** Visit the Giant Buddha in the morning, spend the afternoon eating your way through Zhanggongqiao and the surrounding streets
- **Must-eat order of priority:** Sweet Skin Duck > Qiaojiao Beef > Bobo Ji > Tofu Jelly > Fenzheng Beef
- Many Leshan dishes are available in Chengdu, but locals say the Leshan originals are significantly better
