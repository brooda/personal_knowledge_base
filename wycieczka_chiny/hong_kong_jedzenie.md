# Hong Kong - Przewodnik Kulinarny / Food Guide

## 1. Must-Try Dishes (Dania obowiazkowe)

| Dish | Chinese | Description |
|------|---------|-------------|
| Dim Sum | 點心 | Steamed/fried dumplings and small dishes served with tea (yum cha) |
| Roast Goose | 燒鵝 | Crispy-skinned, succulent goose - HK signature dish |
| Char Siu | 叉燒 | BBQ pork glazed with honey and maltose |
| Wonton Noodles | 雲吞麵 | Thin egg noodles with shrimp wontons in clear broth |
| Egg Waffles | 雞蛋仔 | Crispy-outside, pillowy-inside bubble-shaped waffles |
| Pineapple Bun | 菠蘿包 | Sweet crunchy-topped bun (no pineapple!) - best with cold butter slab inside |
| Milk Tea | 絲襪奶茶 | "Silk stocking" milk tea - strong black tea filtered through cloth |
| Claypot Rice | 煲仔飯 | Rice cooked in clay pot with meats and soy sauce - crispy rice crust on bottom |
| Typhoon Shelter Crab | 避風塘炒蟹 | Crab stir-fried with loads of crispy fried garlic, chilli, black beans |
| Egg Tart | 蛋撻 | Flaky/cookie pastry with smooth egg custard filling |

## 3. Best Roast Goose (Najlepsza pieczona ges)

| Restaurant | Chinese | Location | Michelin | Notes |
|-----------|---------|----------|---------|-------|
| **Yat Lok** | 一樂燒鵝 | G/F, 34-38 Stanley St, Central | 1 Star (since 2015) | Family-run since 1957. Anthony Bourdain: "best roast goose I've ever had." Crispier skin, more unctuous. Mon-Sat 10AM-9PM, Sun 10AM-5:30PM |
| **Kam's Roast Goose** | 甘牌燒鵝 | G/F, 226 Hennessy Rd, Wan Chai | 1 Star | Got Michelin star within 4 months of opening. Cleaner taste, gas-oven roasted. Daily 11:30AM-9:30PM |

**Verdict:** Both excellent. Yat Lok slightly crispier/richer, Kam's cleaner/less greasy. Yat Lok is the classic choice.

## 4. Best Street Food Areas (Najlepsze miejsca na street food)

### Temple Street Night Market (廟街夜市) - Yau Ma Tei
- Open evenings, bustling atmosphere
- Claypot rice, grilled seafood skewers, mango mochi, egg tarts
- Dedicated street food section added recently
- Also has dai pai dong stalls nearby

### Mong Kok (旺角)
- Street food paradise - densest concentration of stalls
- **Fa Yuen Street** and **Dundas Street** especially good for snacks
- Ladies Market area: curry fish balls (咖哩魚蛋), siu mai, stinky tofu (臭豆腐), egg waffles
- Ki Tsui - Michelin-recommended tarts and buns

### Sham Shui Po (深水埗)
- Most affordable area, authentic local vibe
- **Hop Yik Tai** - Michelin-recommended cheung fun (腸粉) - rice noodle rolls with sweet/sesame/soy sauce
- **Kung Wo Tofu Factory** - tofu pudding (豆腐花), tofu puffs, homemade soy milk
- **Mammy Pancake** - egg waffles, 15+ flavours
- Maltose crackers, tofu pudding from street vendors

### Key Street Foods to Try Everywhere
| Food | Chinese | Notes |
|------|---------|-------|
| Curry Fish Balls | 咖哩魚蛋 | Chewy fish balls in spicy curry sauce |
| Egg Waffles | 雞蛋仔 | Classic plain or new flavours (salted egg yolk, matcha) |
| Cheung Fun | 腸粉 | Silky rice noodle rolls with sweet soy |
| Stinky Tofu | 臭豆腐 | Deep-fried fermented tofu - smells bad, tastes great |
| Siu Mai | 燒賣 | Street-style steamed pork dumplings with soy sauce |

## 5. Dai Pai Dong - Open-Air Food Stalls (大牌檔)

**Status:** Only ~17 genuine dai pai dong remain in Hong Kong (down from 200+). Government stopped issuing new licences. Visit them while you still can!

## 6. Cha Chaan Teng - HK-Style Cafes (茶餐廳)

### Must-Visit Classics

| Name | Chinese | District | Must-Order |
|------|---------|----------|-----------|
| **Lan Fong Yuen** | 蘭芳園 | Central | Silk stocking milk tea (original recipe since 1952!), French toast, condensed milk bun |
| **Australia Dairy Company** | 澳洲牛奶公司 | Jordan | Legendary scrambled eggs on buttery toast, macaroni with char siu broth. Expect queues & rushed service |
| **Kam Wah Cafe** | 金華冰廳 | Prince Edward | Best butter-stuffed pineapple bun (菠蘿油) in HK, French toast |
| **Mido Cafe** | 美都餐室 | Yau Ma Tei | Baked spare rib rice with tomato sauce. Retro 1950s interior, great photos |
| **Cheung Hing Coffee Shop** | 祥興咖啡室 | Happy Valley | Since 1951, famous egg tarts and pineapple buns, old wooden booths |
| **Star Cafe** | 星座冰室 | Tsim Sha Tsui | Tomato soup noodles |
| **Tsui Wah** | 翠華餐廳 | Multiple locations | Most famous chain - fishball noodles, Hainanese chicken rice. Open late |

### Essential Cha Chaan Teng Dishes
- **Silk stocking milk tea** (絲襪奶茶) - strong, smooth, the real HK drink
- **Yuenyeung** (鴛鴦) - coffee + tea mixed together
- **Pineapple bun with butter** (菠蘿油) - warm bun with cold butter slab
- **French toast** (西多士) - thick bread, peanut butter, deep-fried, drowned in condensed milk/syrup
- **Macaroni soup** (通粉) - with ham/luncheon meat in broth
- **Scrambled eggs on toast** - Australia Dairy Company style
- **Baked pork chop rice** (焗豬扒飯) - with tomato sauce and cheese

## 8. Quick Reference by District (Szybka referencja wg dzielnicy)

### Central (中環)
- Yat Lok (roast goose), Sing Kee dai pai dong, Lan Fong Yuen, Luk Yu Tea House, Mott 32, Lung King Heen

### Wan Chai (灣仔)
- Kam's Roast Goose, Tung Po dai pai dong, Kui Kee Seafood, Lai's Kitchen

### Jordan / Yau Ma Tei (佐敦/油麻地)
- Temple Street Night Market, Mido Cafe, Wing Fat dai pai dong, Sai Kwan Lo Jo, Mrs. Fong Desserts

### Tsim Sha Tsui (尖沙咀)
- Star Cafe, Lin Heung (new branch), Cheung Hing Kee buns, Din Tai Fung

### Mong Kok (旺角)
- Street food on Fa Yuen St & Dundas St, Ladies Market, Ki Tsui, Twins Liangpi, Joyful Dessert

### Sham Shui Po (深水埗) - BEST FOR BUDGET FOOD
- Tim Ho Wan (original), Hop Yik Tai cheong fun, Kung Wo Tofu, Keung Kee dai pai dong, Oi Man Sing, Block 18 Doggie's Noodle, So Kee, Sun Heung Yuen, Wai Kee Noodle Cafe

### Prince Edward (太子)
- Kam Wah Cafe (pineapple bun!), One Dim Sum
